# The Hill Complex

## gz-hill-01 — The royal acropolis
> location: hill_complex · confidence: established · sources: unesco-whc-364, whe

The Hill Complex crowns a granite spur rising about 80 meters above the valley — the oldest part of the city, occupied continuously from the 11th to the 15th centuries (with traces of sparse habitation as far back as the 5th century). It is generally read as the royal city: the western enclosure is thought to have been the residence of successive rulers, and the eastern enclosure served ritual purposes.

## gz-hill-02 — The Eastern Enclosure and the birds
> location: hill_complex · confidence: established · sources: unesco-whc-364

The Eastern Enclosure of the Hill Complex is where the famous soapstone birds stood: six upright steatite posts topped with carved birds were found here, in the enclosure understood to have served ritual purposes. This was the spiritual heart of the royal hill.

## gz-hill-03 — Walls woven through boulders
> location: hill_complex · confidence: established · sources: unesco-whc-364, smarthistory

On the hill, the builders worked with the landscape rather than against it: walls of granite blocks thread between massive natural boulders, forming distinct enclosures linked by narrow, partly covered passageways. Structures here were ringed by walls up to about 9 meters (30 feet) high, flanked by cylindrical turrets and carved monoliths.

## gz-hill-04 — A sacred hill, then and now
> location: hill_complex · confidence: established · sources: smarthistory, unesco-whc-364, whe

The hill was a place of ritual as well as rule — possibly a religious center for ancestor veneration. A cave on the hill, once associated with the ruler's residence, remains a sacred site for Shona communities today; the site as a whole is still used for spiritual purposes.
