# The Valley Ruins

## gz-valley-01 — Where the city breathed
> location: valley_ruins · confidence: established · sources: whe, unesco-whc-364, libretexts

The Valley Ruins are a scatter of smaller stone enclosures and daga-house remains spread between the hill and the Great Enclosure, occupied mainly in the 14th–16th centuries. Some circular houses here were substantial — up to 10 meters across, with thatched roofs rising around 6 meters. This is where the texture of everyday city life was densest.

## gz-valley-03 — Evidence of a trading hub
> location: valley_ruins · confidence: established · sources: smarthistory

The Valley Ruins yielded some of the clearest evidence that Great Zimbabwe was a hub of long-distance commerce: porcelain fragments from China, beads from southeast Asia, and copper ingots from trading centers along the Zambezi River and Central African kingdoms were all excavated here.

## gz-valley-04 — A city of thousands
> location: valley_ruins · confidence: uncertain · sources: unesco-whc-364, whe, smarthistory, libretexts

How many people lived here? Estimates vary by source. UNESCO records that the city's population exceeded 10,000 at its 14th-century height; other estimates run to about 18,000, and as high as 20,000. Whatever the exact figure, this was an exceptional scale for the region in that era — with most residents living in daga houses beyond the stone walls.
