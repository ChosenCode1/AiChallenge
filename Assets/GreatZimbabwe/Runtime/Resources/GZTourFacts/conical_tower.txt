The Conical Tower is a solid drum of coursed granite, about ten meters tall and five meters across at the base, standing between the Great Enclosure's inner and outer walls.
It has no door, no chamber, and no stair — it is completely solid, symbolic architecture built stone by stone from the ground up.
The favored reading is that it is a giant grain bin rendered in stone: in Shona tradition a ruler shows generosity through his granary, distributing grain as a sign of his protection.
A narrow passage between the parallel walls leads here, so the tower reveals itself only at the end of a tight, high-walled approach.
It was raised with the same mortarless precision as everything around it, and it has held its shape for roughly six centuries.
