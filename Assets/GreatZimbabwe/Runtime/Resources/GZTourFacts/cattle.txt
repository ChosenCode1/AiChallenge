Cattle were the walking wealth of Great Zimbabwe — herds measured status, moved with brides as marriage dowry, and fed the court.
Before gold, there was cattle: managing great herds is what gave the elites their power, and likely what let the Shona expand from farming into mining and long-distance trade.
The large herds that supplied the city moved seasonally between pastures, managed by the royal court itself.
The profits of the famous gold trade ran narrow — there were no public marketplaces, and exchange among the nobility ran on barter — but cattle and farming were the everyday economy everyone lived by.
