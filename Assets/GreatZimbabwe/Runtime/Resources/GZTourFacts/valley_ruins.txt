The Valley Ruins are a scatter of smaller stone enclosures and clay-house remains spread between the hill and the Great Enclosure, at their busiest in the 14th to 16th centuries.
This is where the texture of city life was densest: round daga homes — clay over wooden frames — some 10 meters across with thatched roofs rising around 6 meters.
Finds from these enclosures prove the city's global reach: porcelain from China, beads from southeast Asia, and copper ingots from trading centers along the Zambezi.
At its height the whole city held over ten thousand people — some estimates run to eighteen or even twenty thousand — most living in daga homes beyond the stone walls.
