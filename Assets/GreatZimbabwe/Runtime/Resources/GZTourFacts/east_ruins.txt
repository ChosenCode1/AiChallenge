The East Ruins are smaller stone enclosures on the eastern side of the valley — part of the wider sprawl of a city whose ruins span some 730 hectares, over seven square kilometers.
Great Zimbabwe was never just its monuments: it was a whole living town, and each cluster of walls like this insulated a living ensemble — a family's own compound of huts, floors, and benches.
The same dry-stone craft appears here as in the great walls — coursed granite laid without mortar — applied at the scale of a household rather than a kingdom.
Beyond these walls stretched the daga-house neighborhoods where most of the city's thousands actually lived, of which little remains above ground.
